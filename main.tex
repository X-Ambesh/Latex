\section*{Mathematical Content}

Let $f(x) = \sin x$ be a continuous function on the real line $\mathbb{R}$.  
The derivative of   $\frac{d f(x)}{dx}$   is given by:
\begin{equation}
\frac{d}{dx}(f(x)) = \cos x
\end{equation}

From equation (1), we observe that the rate of change of $f(x)$ depends on the cosine function.
